Statuses & Inflictions

Mechanical, self-contained triggers built around the Major / Minor / Neutral / Weak Spot tiers.
Injuries
Physical, usually from combat or danger — clear when treated or rested.
InjuryEffectClears When
Twisted AnkleDowngrade one tier on anything requiring running, climbing, or quick footworkResting a scene, or splinted
ConcussionDowngrade one tier on anything requiring focus/memory/clear thinkingSleeping it off, or treated
Broken FingerDowngrade one tier on anything requiring fine motor control (locks, phones, tools)Splinted/taped
Deep CutTake 1 Bleeding stack (see below)Bandaged (Lifeguard skill auto-clears)
WindedLose your bonus die (skill/Minor/Major) on your next physical actionOne full scene passes
Burned HandDowngrade one tier on anything using that handTreated, or 1 full scene
Bad FallDowngrade one tier on all physical checks until treatedRested + treated
Bleeding — a stacking status, not a one-time injury: each stack forces one extra "roll 2d6, keep the lowest" die into your next physical roll, stacking additively. Bandaging removes all stacks at once.
Statuses
Temporary, often supernatural — cleared by a specific in-fiction action, not just time.
StatusEffectClears When
TerrifiedDowngrade one tier on anything while the source of fear is present/visibleThe source leaves, is dealt with, or hidden from
FrozenSkip your next action entirelySomeone else acts to snap you out of it
Adrenaline RushUpgrade your next roll one tierUsed once, then expires
Cursed (temp.)Once, the GM may force a downgrade on a roll of their choiceTriggers once, then clears
Possessed (brief)GM narrates your next action instead of youEnd of the GM's chosen beat
HallucinatingGM inserts one false detail into the scene you must treat as realEnd of scene, or if disbelieved by another PC
IsolatedCan't receive help/assist actions from other PCsPhysically reunite with the group
MarkedGM can have something locate you once per session, no roll neededSession ends, or the mark is removed in-fiction
NumbImmune to Squeamish/Germaphobe-type Flaw triggers, but also don't notice new Injuries when they happenFeeling returns (GM/player call)
Emotional
Concrete versions — each has a specific trigger and a specific fix, not just flavor.
ConditionEffectClears When
Panic SpiralAfter any Critical Failure, your very next roll auto-downgrades one tier tooThat next roll resolves (good or bad)
Grief-StruckDowngrade one tier whenever the GM/player invokes the specific lossConfronted directly, or scene ends
DespairCap your rolls at Minor tier — Major bonus doesn't apply even if you have itA hope-restoring beat (GM or player-triggered)
RageUpgrade Brawns-type rolls one tier, downgrade Common Sense-type rolls one tierScene ends, or deliberately calmed
OverwhelmedIf 2+ statuses/flaws are active at once, add a 3rd automatic downgrade on topWhenever you're down to 1 or 0 active statuses
Hesitation (Guilt)If you choose self-preservation over helping someone, downgrade your very next rollResolves after that one roll
Design Notes
  • Everything has an exit. Nothing here is permanent or purely additive punishment — every entry has a listed way to clear it. Players need to feel like they can do something about their condition, not just suffer passively.
  • Bleeding is the death spiral. It's the one status that gets worse if ignored — everything else is a flat, single-tier hit.
  • Overwhelmed is the safety valve. Flaws already trigger tier-downgrades, so a character who's Bleeding and Terrified and has their Flaw active could stack three downgrades. Overwhelmed just names that moment explicitly — and it's a great horror beat ("everything is going wrong at once").
