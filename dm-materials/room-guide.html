Room-by-Room Guide

A linear chain of rooms, each with a door on the far side and more than one way to reach it.
The Barker. Since the cast is thin on NPCs, one voice carries the whole dimension — a Ringmaster/Barker who's never physically present, only heard through speakers, funhouse mirrors, and cue cards. He taunts, lies, and occasionally helps if it amuses him. He shows himself, in person, only at the very end.
0Cold Open — The Hayride

Mundane wagon ride goes wrong: the tractor dies in fog, the scare-actor scarecrows are standing in formation facing the wagon, and the trail behind them is gone. The haunted house is the only structure still visible.

No obstacle — this is the hook that gets them walking toward the door. Nothing to solve, just somewhere to go.

1The Lobby That Forgot How To Be a Lobby

Ticket booth, gift shop shelves gone dark — replaced by three carnival game stalls, lights flickering, no attendants in sight. The exit door at the far wall has three empty keyholes.

Obstacle: the door needs all three keys. Stalls can be tackled in any order, and each one can be won more than one way.

Ring the BellKey 1

A high striker with a bell shaped like a ribcage. The puck is already halfway up, like someone got close and stopped.

  • BrawnsSwing hard enough to send the puck the rest of the way — works, but ringing it fully echoes into the next room and tips something off early.
  • BrainsSpot the rigged counterweight bolted behind the tower and disable it instead of overpowering the machine.
  • Common SenseThe puck is balanced right at the tipping point — a light tap does what a hard swing would, no strength roll needed.
  • Wildcard / LuckyA player narrates a plan and it just works — no roll, just conviction.
Tin Skull GalleryKey 2

Grinning tin skulls glide back and forth behind the counter. One has a tiny keyhole where an eye should be.

  • BrainsTrack the skulls' pattern and call the shot on the one carrying the key-eye.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyReposition along the counter fast enough to always have an angle before it passes.
  • Common Sense / AV ClubNotice the control box under the counter and just flip the switch to stop the skulls, then pick the key at leisure.
  • Beauty / Class ClownCharm or distract the broken fortune-teller animatronic overseeing the booth into pointing out which skull.
The Duck PondKey 3

Rubber ducks bob in murky water. One has a scratch shaped like a key.

  • Common SenseThe marked duck drifts against the current, unlike the rest — watch before grabbing.
  • BrawnsTip the whole pond over and let the water — and ducks — spill onto the floor, then grab the right one.
  • Beauty / Early BloomerThe ducks turn to face whoever seems friendliest — coax the marked one over instead of chasing it.
  • Chem WhizNotice the water's unnaturally still because of an additive, and drain or neutralize it to force every duck to the surface.
Complication: failing a game doesn't lock the party out — it just costs a scene and the Barker gets a little nastier over the speakers. As always, enough Brawns can skip the room entirely by tearing the door off its frame instead of collecting all three keys — messier, and worth an Injury roll.
2The Hall of Mirrors

Every mirror shows a slightly wrong version of the viewer — aged, injured, or doing something they haven't done yet. The layout never looks quite the same twice.

Obstacle: the mirrors reposition themselves whenever nobody's looking directly at them, turning the hall into a maze that can't be memorized — only whoever last looked away knows it moved.

Secret: each mirror is being carried by an undead minion, silent and still as long as it's watched. Breaking a mirror, or catching one mid-shift, exposes the thing underneath — and it will grab or block whoever exposed it to keep the maze closed.
  • BrainsKeep something on every mirror at once — a phone propped to record, mirrors angled to watch other mirrors, the party splitting to cross-watch — so nothing can move unobserved. Freezes the maze in place.
  • Common SenseThe minions aren't silent — footsteps, dragging, breathing. Follow the sound instead of the sight to find the gaps without needing to catch anything moving.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyCatch a mirror mid-shift and slip through the half-second gap before it reseals — fast enough that nothing gets a grip.
  • Brawns / HooliganSmash straight through. Shatters the mirror, exposes the minion carrying it, and turns the puzzle into a straight scramble to the door.
Complication: an exposed minion doesn't chase far or fight hard — it just grabs. Getting caught triggers Frozen, or Isolated if it happens while separated from the group.
3The Big Top

The next room is a sagging circus tent packed with a floor show that never stops. Acrobats loop the same dead swing forever, a strongman lifts the same rusted barbell, a tightrope walker missteps and catches herself in the exact same place every time. Nothing here breathes.

Obstacle: there's no door at the far end — the only way out is a tear at the peak of the tent, high above the ring, with the whole show happening directly underneath it.

The Ringleader. A gaunt figure in a rotted ringmaster's coat stands center ring, conducting the performers with slow, precise gestures like a puppeteer. He doesn't chase — he directs. Disrupt the loop too obviously and he turns his baton, and every performer's attention, toward whoever did it.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyTime a leap onto a trapeze mid-swing and ride its arc up to the tear, matching the acrobats' loop instead of fighting it.
  • BrawnsSkip the show entirely and climb the support rigging or tent pole hand over hand.
  • Brains / Movie BuffClock the exact repeating loop and slip through a stagehand's catwalk that the performers habitually ignore.
  • Beauty / Theater Kid / Class ClownJoin the act as a "volunteer" — sell it well enough that the Ringleader directs the cannon, lift, or trapeze to feature you, launching you toward the tear as part of the show.
  • Wildcard / Explosives / HooliganRig something loud to break the loop and open a gap in the crowd — fast, but the noise turns the Ringleader's baton straight at whoever caused it.
Complication: if the Ringleader notices, every performer converges on the interruption at once — a fast, overwhelming grab rather than a fight. Getting caught risks Isolated, or Overwhelmed if other statuses are already active.
4Clown Alley

A funhouse corridor of identical clown mannequins. One is real and will follow at the exact same slow pace as the party unless someone stops moving to check.

Obstacle: this is a tension room, not a combat room — the threat is psychological, not physical.

  • BrainsCount/pattern-match the mannequins to spot the one out of sequence.
  • BackflipsCreate distance fast enough that pace-matching becomes impossible, then double back once ahead.
  • Beauty / Theater KidMimic the clown's exact walk and blend in as one of them to slip past unnoticed.
  • Common SenseJust don't stop moving, ever, no matter what — bulldoze through the dread with discipline.
Complication: stopping to check is a natural spot for a Coward or Insomniac Flaw to trigger.
5The Talent Show

Past the tent, a narrow hallway dead-ends into a cramped prep room — cracked mirrors, a costume rack of ill-fitting outfits, a call sheet with the party's names already on it in handwriting that isn't theirs.

The Usher. A female clown in smudged greasepaint meets them here, both eyes drifting slightly out of sync, never quite settling on whoever she's talking to. She explains the rules like it's the most normal thing in the world: they're on next, the judges decide who leaves, and the trapdoor behind the judges' table only opens for an act that wins them over.

Obstacle: onstage, a panel of three bizarre judges sits behind the table. The trapdoor stays sealed until at least two of the three approve — and each judge wants something different.

The ProfessorVote 1

Stitched together from book pages and rulers, monocle screwed on sideways. Wants proof of cleverness.

  • BrainsSolve or explain something clever on the spot — a riddle, a trick, a walkthrough of how an earlier room's puzzle actually worked.
  • Movie Buff / AV ClubRecreate a slick trick or bit of stagecraft borrowed wholesale from a movie — he applauds the reference.
  • Common SensePoint out the contradiction in his own scoring rubric — he respects being correctly argued with more than being entertained.
The StarletVote 2

An eyeless, glittering mannequin obsessed with elegance and drama. Wants a show, not a skill.

  • Beauty / Theater KidA full dramatic number — monologue, song, dance — sold with total commitment.
  • DancingA genuine dance number wins her over on technique alone.
  • Class ClownLean into an exaggerated comedy bit instead of grace — she rewards commitment to a bit just as much as elegance.
The WreckVote 3

Hulking, scarred, missing an arm. Wants danger, spectacle, a little pain.

  • BrawnsA feat of real strength — breaking, lifting, or throwing something the room clearly doesn't want moved.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyA dangerous acrobatic stunt, the riskier the better.
  • Explosives / HooliganA loud, dangerous flourish — thrilling if it works, worth an Injury roll if it doesn't.
Complication: failing to win a majority doesn't end the act — the Usher just says "again" and the show restarts, colder and stranger each time. Enough Brawns can force the trapdoor without any votes, but doing so turns every judge and the dead audience against the party at once — a much bigger Overwhelmed than skipping any other room's puzzle.
6The Chancel

A ritual chamber — pews facing an altar. The final door is behind it, but the altar demands a trade: something be left behind before it opens.

Obstacle: an item, a memory, or a lie admitted aloud — the altar wants something given up.

  • BrainsFind the loophole in the ritual's wording — it wants something given up, not necessarily something valuable.
  • Beauty / Class ClownTalk the room into accepting a performance or confession as the trade instead of an object.
  • Common SenseSacrifice a prop/decoy item deliberately brought along earlier — rewards foresight from Rooms 1–3.
Complication: if nobody's willing to give anything up, this is a natural spot for Despair or Grief-Struck to trigger instead of a hard fail.
7Finale — The Threshold Home

The exit door is real, but the Barker finally shows himself here — not as a fight, but as a final negotiation/chase.

Obstacle: no new attribute gate. Every earlier room's choice — what they left in the Chancel, who's Bleeding, who's Overwhelmed — determines how hard this is. Let accumulated Statuses/Injuries pay off mechanically instead of adding a new puzzle.
