Room-by-Room Guide

A linear chain of rooms, each with a door on the far side and more than one way to reach it.
The Barker. Since the cast is thin on NPCs, one voice carries the whole dimension — a Ringmaster/Barker who's never physically present, only heard through speakers, funhouse mirrors, and cue cards. He taunts, lies, and occasionally helps if it amuses him. He shows himself, in person, only at the very end.
0Cold Open — The Hayride

Mundane wagon ride goes wrong: the tractor dies in fog, the scare-actor scarecrows are standing in formation facing the wagon, and the trail behind them is gone. The haunted house is the only structure still visible.

No obstacle — this is the hook that gets them walking toward the door. Nothing to solve, just somewhere to go.

1The Lobby That Forgot How To Be a Lobby

Ticket booth, gift shop shelves gone dark — replaced by three carnival game stalls, lights flickering, no attendants in sight. The exit door at the far wall has three empty keyholes.

Obstacle: the door needs all three keys. Stalls can be tackled in any order, and each one can be won more than one way.

Ring the BellKey 1

A high striker with a bell shaped like a ribcage. The puck is already halfway up, like someone got close and stopped.

  • BrawnsSwing hard enough to send the puck the rest of the way — works, but ringing it fully echoes into the next room and tips something off early.
  • BrainsSpot the rigged counterweight bolted behind the tower and disable it instead of overpowering the machine.
  • Common SenseThe puck is balanced right at the tipping point — a light tap does what a hard swing would, no strength roll needed.
  • Wildcard / LuckyA player narrates a plan and it just works — no roll, just conviction.
Tin Skull GalleryKey 2

Grinning tin skulls glide back and forth behind the counter. One has a tiny keyhole where an eye should be.

  • BrainsTrack the skulls' pattern and call the shot on the one carrying the key-eye.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyReposition along the counter fast enough to always have an angle before it passes.
  • Common Sense / AV ClubNotice the control box under the counter and just flip the switch to stop the skulls, then pick the key at leisure.
  • Beauty / Class ClownCharm or distract the broken fortune-teller animatronic overseeing the booth into pointing out which skull.
The Duck PondKey 3

Rubber ducks bob in murky water. One has a scratch shaped like a key.

  • Common SenseThe marked duck drifts against the current, unlike the rest — watch before grabbing.
  • BrawnsTip the whole pond over and let the water — and ducks — spill onto the floor, then grab the right one.
  • Beauty / Early BloomerThe ducks turn to face whoever seems friendliest — coax the marked one over instead of chasing it.
  • Chem WhizNotice the water's unnaturally still because of an additive, and drain or neutralize it to force every duck to the surface.
Complication: failing a game doesn't lock the party out — it just costs a scene and the Barker gets a little nastier over the speakers. As always, enough Brawns can skip the room entirely by tearing the door off its frame instead of collecting all three keys — messier, and worth an Injury roll.
2The Hall of Mirrors

Every mirror shows a slightly wrong version of the viewer — aged, injured, or doing something they haven't done yet.

Obstacle: one mirror is the real exit; the rest are dead ends that trap whoever steps through.

  • BrainsThe true exit is the only mirror where the reflection is out of sync with them by half a second.
  • Backflips / SkaterboyVault along the mirror frames without touching the glass to reach the far one blind.
  • Common Sense / Eagle ScoutMark reflections (chalk, tape) to eliminate mirrors by process of elimination instead of guessing.
  • Beauty / Early BloomerTheir reflection is the only one that visibly wants them to come closer — trust vanity over logic.
Complication: stepping into a wrong mirror triggers Frozen, or Isolated if they're alone when it happens.
3The Butcher's Pantry

A kitchen/meat-locker smell room. The next door is behind a hanging curtain of meat hooks and carcasses — obviously fake haunted-house props, except one is real, and it's watching.

Obstacle: get through the curtain without confronting what's actually in it (Squeamish-flavored gauntlet).

  • BrawnsMuscle straight through the hooks, risking a Snag (treat as Deep Cut) but fast.
  • Chem WhizIdentify the preservative smell that reveals which "carcass" is the fake threat vs. the real one.
  • Common Sense / LifeguardMethodically check for a pulse/breathing before committing to a path — slow but safe.
  • Wildcard / Class ClownLoudly narrate the room like a cooking show to keep morale up and power through without looking closely — avoids the Squeamish trigger by not truly confronting it.
4Clown Alley

A funhouse corridor of identical clown mannequins. One is real and will follow at the exact same slow pace as the party unless someone stops moving to check.

Obstacle: this is a tension room, not a combat room — the threat is psychological, not physical.

  • BrainsCount/pattern-match the mannequins to spot the one out of sequence.
  • BackflipsCreate distance fast enough that pace-matching becomes impossible, then double back once ahead.
  • Beauty / Theater KidMimic the clown's exact walk and blend in as one of them to slip past unnoticed.
  • Common SenseJust don't stop moving, ever, no matter what — bulldoze through the dread with discipline.
Complication: stopping to check is a natural spot for a Coward or Insomniac Flaw to trigger.
5The Bone Orchard

The hayride field again, except now endless and lit by a moon that doesn't move. Scarecrows are posted at regular intervals, each one a landmark.

Obstacle: the exit is one specific scarecrow, but they all look identical from a distance.

  • Common Sense / Eagle ScoutNavigate by a consistent detail (wind direction, crow behavior, footprints) instead of sight.
  • Brains / AV ClubUse a phone compass/flashlight trick to detect the one "scarecrow" that's actually a broadcasting prop — the Barker's mouthpiece.
  • Brawns / HooliganKnock scarecrows down one by one until the field "notices" and reacts, revealing the real exit by process of elimination — loud, risky, may draw something.
  • Wildcard / LuckyLet a player narrate a hunch and just be right — no roll, just conviction.
6The Chancel

A ritual chamber — pews facing an altar. The final door is behind it, but the altar demands a trade: something be left behind before it opens.

Obstacle: an item, a memory, or a lie admitted aloud — the altar wants something given up.

  • BrainsFind the loophole in the ritual's wording — it wants something given up, not necessarily something valuable.
  • Beauty / Class ClownTalk the room into accepting a performance or confession as the trade instead of an object.
  • Common SenseSacrifice a prop/decoy item deliberately brought along earlier — rewards foresight from Rooms 1–3.
Complication: if nobody's willing to give anything up, this is a natural spot for Despair or Grief-Struck to trigger instead of a hard fail.
7Finale — The Threshold Home

The exit door is real, but the Barker finally shows himself here — not as a fight, but as a final negotiation/chase.

Obstacle: no new attribute gate. Every earlier room's choice — what they left in the Chancel, who's Bleeding, who's Overwhelmed — determines how hard this is. Let accumulated Statuses/Injuries pay off mechanically instead of adding a new puzzle.
